Unemployment Rate Analysis

Dataset

Dataset was obtained from the Federal Reserve Economic Data and retrieved using Fred API in Python. Two (2) data sets were used for this analysis:

  • Unemployment rate: The unemployment rate represents the number of unemployed as a percentage of the labor force. Labor force data are restricted to people 16 years of age and older, who currently reside in 1 of the 50 states or the District of Columbia, who do not reside in institutions (e.g., penal and mental facilities, homes for the aged), and who are not on active duty in the Armed Forces.
  • Participation rate: The Labor Force Participation Rate is defined by the Current Population Survey (CPS) as “the number of people in the labor force as a percentage of the civilian noninstitutional population. Therefore, the participation rate is the percentage of the population that is either working or actively looking for work.”

    Objective

    The goal of this project was to analyze the unemployment rate versus the participation rate across the U.S. States and get insights such as tendency and behavior over the years.
